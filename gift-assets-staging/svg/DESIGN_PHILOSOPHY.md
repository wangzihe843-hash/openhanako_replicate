# Warm Inventory（温柔库存）

**Movement**: Warm Inventory — the art of the cherished object at rest.

Every artifact in this collection is a single object suspended in warm, breathable cream space — an item lifted out of its world and placed gently at the center of attention, the way a giver holds a gift in two hands before offering it. The canvas is not a scene; it is a velvet tray. Nothing competes with the object. The background is one unbroken field of soft cream (#FAF3E6), and the only concession to gravity is a faint warm shadow pooling beneath the object, grounding it without dramatizing it.

Form is flat, confident, and quietly meticulous. Shapes are cut clean as paper craft: no gradients doing the work of draftsmanship, no outlines louder than the silhouette itself. Depth arrives only through same-hue shading — a darker petal behind a lighter one, a shadowed flank on a wooden handle — each tone step small enough to feel like lacquer layers applied by a patient hand. The object should read instantly at thumbnail size and reward close viewing at full size; that double legibility is the craft, and it must look labored over, the product of countless refinements by a master of reduction.

Color is a warm domestic palette: creams, kraft browns, muted rose, sage, faded vermilion, soft gold. Saturation stays low and cozy — colors that have been washed and dried in the sun. One accent of red or pink is permitted per object, placed where the eye should land first. Cold pure hues, neon, and pitch black are foreign to this world; the darkest dark is a warm umber.

Composition is centered and frontal with the patience of a museum catalog plate. The object occupies roughly half the canvas height, breathing room on all sides, optical center slightly above geometric center. Symmetry is embraced where the object offers it and broken only by honest asymmetries of the thing itself — a tassel swinging slightly, a branch crossing a fan face. Every curve must look intentional, every interval measured.

Text does not exist here. Not a letter, not a numeral, not a maker's mark. The object alone carries all meaning — these are icons of affection, and affection does not caption itself. The work must stand as if each piece took hours of careful cutting and placing: meticulously crafted, painstakingly aligned, unmistakably the work of expert hands.
